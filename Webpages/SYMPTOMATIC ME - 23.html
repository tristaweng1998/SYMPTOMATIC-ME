23. Unuttered

All these brings me to remember George Floyd, who was murdered after a brutal police officer pressed his knees to his neck for 9min and 29sec, and whose dying words, “I can't breathe”, so sincere and desperate, became a rallying cry for a new wave of BLM protests.

In China, nevertheless, such tragedies or atrocities happen every day and everywhere under the Zero-Covid Policy. But they have never promoted the force of a united people, only died away in the sufferings of the victims. I take the reason being more than the fact that they are censored, but that the victims didn't utter some powerful words.

Probably it is because of the language, as the Chinese synonym for “I can't breathe” is written as “我不能呼吸”, a line consisting of five characters with different pronunciations, which is difficult for a tortured person to utter at once. All he could say is “救命”, which means “Help!”, a phrase too common to be used as a slogan of protest.

But in many cases, even “Help!” was not uttered, and we just see the victim being in silence, as if he has already accepted his punishment.

So did he deserve it? Did he deserve to be punished for his unuttered-ness while being punished in such an unfair way?
