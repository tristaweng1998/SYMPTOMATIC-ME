15. Blood

Are biological relationships, bonds by blood, that important? Are we bound to love those whom we are bound by blood, or can we choose to love those we should not?

This reminds me of my boss, my teacher, and my father of choice, Su Qing, an independent filmmaker, a musician, and a restaurateur whose family relationships, including his mother, three siblings, son, partner, and me, are all unrelated by blood.

These kinds of relationships, something undefined between nullity and obligation, give him the greatest freedom to decide how and how much responsibility he should assume. But as far as I can see, he has chosen to see them as an obligation, even though he doesn't love his family out of that obligation.

In Chinese, there is a word that writes "萍水", Ping Shui, which describes strangers meeting by coincidence like patches of drifting duckweed. And in many stories, an encounter of Ping Shui is the beginning of a true friendship of destiny.

Shi Jun, a doctor at a hospital in Heilongjiang province, committed suicide in April 2022 after admitting a patient for surgery who later tested positive for Covid. He was accused of spreading the virus and was interrogated for seven days in all humiliation.
He left his elderly mother, wife and twin daughters who were still in middle school.
