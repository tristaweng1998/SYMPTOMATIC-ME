16. One Child Family

Both of my biological parents are university teachers. In China, this means that you work “within the system”, i.e., in a state-owned institution. A job like this guarantees you a stable salary and reliable social benefits for life, unless you hit the "red line" that goes against the will of the Chinese Communist Party.

The year I was born, 1998, was during the strictest period of the One-Child Policy. So, legally, if my parents had a second child, they would both lose their jobs.

But I think that's quite a different story when I asked my parents why they didn't give me a sibling, they replied without hesitation that if they did, they would both lose their jobs.

My question is, is it simply because they themselves didn't want a second child that they put the blame on the One-Child Policy, or did they pass up the opportunity to have a second child because they were afraid of the One-Child Policy? Or somewhere in between, did they not even think about having a second child because of the mere knowledge of the One-Child Policy?

Either way, the past is in the past, and the reality is I am the only child whose growing up was steeped in inevitable loneliness, for I didn't have enough blood relations to rely on. Thus I learned to be independent at a very young age, and today I am still learning to be dependent.
