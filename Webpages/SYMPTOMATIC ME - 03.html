3. Choice of Mine

Here my name is Trista and I was born in China.
Apparently, Trista is not my original name, namely not the name given to me by my family of origin.

My original name is 笑笑, "xiaoxiao", which means “laugh and laugh". I rarely use this name outside of my homeland because I feel it is difficult to pronounce it correctly for non-native Chinese speakers .

So I picked a name, "Trista", whose meaning I found on the Chinese Internet, "a girl who hides her sorrows with a smile". I liked it as I saw it doesn't stray too far from my original name, while reflecting some of my hidden realities.

But when I came here and searched for its meaning again, all I was told was that it means "deep sorrows", the exact opposite of my original name.

Still, I chose to keep the name.

In Zero-Covid China, your Covid code marks the “clearness” of your identity.
A green code means you are safe with others, and a red code means you should be cleared away.
